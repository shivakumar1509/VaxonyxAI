Process of Protein Production

Protein production—including expression and purification—is a recombinant DNA–enabled workflow that generates large quantities of a specific protein for research, diagnostics, therapeutics, and industrial use.

Expression Platforms

🧬

Mammalian (HEK293, CHO)

Suspension cultures for large-scale recombinant proteins and antibodies (intracellular, secreted, or membrane).

  • Transient transfection, BacMam transduction
  • Stable cell line development for reproducibility
🪲

Insect (Baculovirus)

High yields and fast turnaround with near-mammalian PTMs once virus stocks are prepared.

  • sf9/sf21 or High-Five® cells
  • Scale-up ready after P2/P3 amplification
🧫

Bacterial (E. coli)

Reliable, reproducible production using diverse strains at bioreactor scale.

  • Up to ~40 L per batch
  • Periplasmic & inclusion-body strategies
🍞

Yeast (e.g., Pichia)

High-yield expression with mammalian-like modifications; strong secretory system and easy genetics.

  • Methanol-induced and constitutive promoters
  • Excellent for glycoproteins difficult in bacteria

Process of Protein Production

Protein production, including expression and purification, is the biotechnical generation of specific proteins using recombinant DNA technology. This process supports applications across diagnostics, therapeutics, research, and industrial biotechnology.

Protein Expression

We provide multiple expression systems — mammalian, insect, bacterial, and yeast — each optimized to project-specific requirements.

Mammalian Expression

HEK293 and CHO suspension cultures enable large-scale recombinant protein and antibody production. Using plasmid transfection, BacMam viral transduction, or stable cell lines, we achieve high-yield and reproducible expression for intracellular, secreted, and membrane proteins.

Insect Expression

Baculovirus-mediated expression in insect cells supports scalable production of intracellular, secreted, or membrane proteins with mammalian-like post-translational modifications and fast turnaround.

Bacterial Expression

E. coli-based systems offer reliable and reproducible protein expression at bioreactor scales up to 40 L per batch.

Yeast Expression

Yeast systems combine scalability with near-mammalian post-translational modifications, ideal for complex proteins. Genetic modification enables functional versions of therapeutically relevant proteins.

Protein Purification

Protein purification isolates target proteins using physicochemical properties like size, charge, and hydrophobicity. Achieving >90% purity ensures suitability for structural and functional studies.
